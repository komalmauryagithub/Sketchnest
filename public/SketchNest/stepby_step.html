Step 1: Know Your Tools

  • Use different pencils (HB, 2B, 4B, etc.)
  • Learn about erasers (kneaded, hard), paper types, and sharpeners
  • Practice light and dark strokes

Step 2: Basic Line Practice

  • Draw straight, curved, zig-zag, wavy lines
  • Do it slowly, repeatedly for hand control

Step 3: Draw Simple Shapes

  • Sketch circles, ovals, squares, rectangles, triangles
  • Maintain proportions & clean edges

Step 4: Understand Shading

  • Techniques: hatching, cross-hatching, blending, stippling
  • Practice gradients from light to dark

Step 5: Combine Shapes

  • Draw objects by combining shapes (e.g., a snowman, cup, tree)
  • Learn structure using basic forms

  • 1-point, 2-point, and 3-point perspectives
  • Draw roads, buildings, boxes in 3D

Step 7: Still-Life Sketching

  • Sketch real objects (mug, lamp, plant)
  • Focus on proportions, angles, lighting

Step 8: Human Proportions

  • Learn facial guidelines: eyes halfway, nose, mouth alignment
  • Full-body sketching: 7‚Äì8 head-tall figure
  • Practice hands, feet, eyes, noses

Step 9: Texture Studies

  • Draw texture of wood, stone, metal, fur, cloth

üé® PHASE 3: Advanced (Detail & Creativity)

Step 10: Lighting & Shadows

  • Learn cast shadows vs. form shadows
  • Observe light direction and apply it to sketches

Step 11: Dynamic Poses & Motion

  • Practice gesture drawing
  • Draw figures in motion (running, dancing)

Step 12: Detailed Realism

  • Try hyperrealism (eye, skin, hair strands)
  • Use blending tools (tissue, brush, stump)

Step 13: Character Design / Stylization

  • Develop your art style
  • Create original characters or stylize portraits

Step 14: Final Projects

  • Sketch a complete scene, story panel, or portrait
  • Include foreground, midground, background, and light source
